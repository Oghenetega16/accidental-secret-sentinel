Privacy Policy

Accidental Secret Sentinel Chrome Extension  ·  Last updated: May 2026

TL;DR — This extension processes everything locally in your browser. No data is ever sent to any server. No analytics. No telemetry.

What we collect

Accidental Secret Sentinel collects nothing. The extension:

  • Does not transmit any scanned data outside your browser
  • Does not log, store, or upload findings to any server
  • Does not collect analytics, crash reports, or usage telemetry
  • Does not use cookies or third-party tracking
  • Does not communicate with any external service or API

How scanning works

All secret detection runs entirely within your browser using Chrome extension APIs. The extension:

  • Intercepts network requests and responses locally, before they leave or enter your browser
  • Scans JavaScript bundles and HTML source as they are loaded into your tab
  • Stores findings temporarily in chrome.storage.local — a sandboxed, browser-local store that no external party can access
  • Stores your suppression list and settings in chrome.storage.sync, which Chrome may sync across your own signed-in devices via Google's infrastructure

Permissions used and why

  • storage — to save findings and your suppression list locally
  • scripting — to inject the content script that intercepts page-level requests
  • activeTab — to read the current tab's URL for domain suppression checks
  • webRequest — to observe network request metadata (headers only, not bodies; bodies are read via the page-context content script)
  • <all_urls> — required to scan requests and page content across all sites you visit

Data retention

Findings are stored only for the duration of your browser session on a given tab. They are automatically cleared when you navigate away from a page or close the tab. Your suppression list persists until you manually remove entries via the Settings page.

Third parties

This extension has no third-party dependencies that make network requests. All pattern matching logic is bundled locally.

Contact

If you have questions about this privacy policy, please open an issue on the project's GitHub repository or contact the developer directly via the Chrome Web Store listing page.
